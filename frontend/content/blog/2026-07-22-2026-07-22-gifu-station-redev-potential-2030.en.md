Against the backdrop of the expanding Nagoya metropolitan area and the Chuo Shinkansen maglev line project, cities once considered mere "commuter towns" for Nagoya are seeing their potential re-evaluated. Among them, the area around Gifu Station, the heart of Gifu City—one of the major urban centers in the Tokai region—is drawing intense interest from real estate investors due to large-scale redevelopment projects and excellent transportation links. But does the Gifu Station area truly represent a promising investment destination looking toward 2030?

This article, from the perspective of a veteran real estate analyst, provides a thorough analysis of the redevelopment projects around Gifu Station, the latest real estate transaction data, and its advantages as part of the Nagoya commuter belt. Based on public data, we will uncover the true value and potential risks of this area, offering insights into wise investment strategies for 2026 and beyond.

## 1. Why are investors now focusing on Gifu Station?

The growing interest in Gifu Station is about more than just geographical proximity to Nagoya. The area's true potential lies at the intersection of two major trends: the "restructuring of urban functions" and its role as a "hub in the wider transportation network."

First, Gifu City is promoting a "compact city" model centered around the station, with several redevelopment projects already underway or completed. This has led to a concentration of commercial facilities, offices, and high-rise condominiums, dramatically enhancing the city's appeal and convenience. This serves as a powerful driver, attracting new residents and businesses and bolstering real estate demand.

Second, the excellent access to Nagoya Station—just about 20 minutes on the JR Tokaido Main Line—is gaining renewed appreciation as hybrid work styles combining remote and in-office work become the norm. The area's more affordable property prices compared to central Nagoya are a significant draw for those seeking a higher quality of life.

In fact, data from Mekiki Research between 2021 and 2025 confirms 6,249 real estate transactions in the Gifu Station vicinity, indicating a highly active market. This bustling activity is clear evidence that anticipation for redevelopment and genuine demand are working in tandem to drive the market forward.

## 2. Overview of ongoing and completed redevelopment projects around Gifu Station

The recent redevelopment projects are essential to understanding the value of the Gifu Station area. These initiatives have transformed the station's cityscape and created new flows of people.

*   Redevelopment of the Gifu Station North Exit Plaza:
    By expanding pedestrian spaces and installing stylishly designed canopies, the plaza's function as an inviting "face of the city" has been strengthened. Its convenience as a public transportation hub has also improved, significantly elevating the sense of urban sophistication from the moment one steps off the train.

*   Redevelopment of Gifu City Tower 43 and the Tonyamachi-Nishi District:
    Soaring to the west of the station, Gifu City Tower 43 is a mixed-use complex of commercial, public, and residential facilities that has become a local landmark. Following this, redevelopment in the adjacent Tonyamachi-Nishi district has brought new high-rise condominiums and commercial buildings, playing a major role in accommodating the demand for urban living.

*   Revitalization of the Yanagase District:
    In Yanagase, Gifu's iconic shopping arcade, efforts are underway to replace aging structures and attract new commercial tenants. Notably, "Yanagase GRASSEL 35," a mixed-use complex of retail, public, and residential spaces built on the former site of the Nagasakiya department store, has brought new vitality to the area.

These redevelopments are not merely about updating buildings. Through enhancing pedestrian networks, creating green spaces, and strategically placing cultural facilities, they contribute to "creating a walkable city." This, in turn, serves as the engine for long-term growth in real estate value.

## 4. Appeal as a Nagoya commuter town and transportation advantages

Gifu Station's greatest strength is undoubtedly its outstanding access to Nagoya. From Gifu Station, operated by the Central Japan Railway Company (JR Central), a Special Rapid train on the Tokaido Main Line will get you to Nagoya Station in just 19 minutes. In some cases, this is even faster than commuting from suburban areas within Nagoya City itself.

This "short travel time" directly translates into real estate value. For people working in central Nagoya, the Gifu Station area can be a "top choice" for a place to live. Especially as property prices in Nagoya continue to soar, the relative appeal of the Gifu area—where one can obtain a more spacious, higher-quality living environment for the same commute time—is only increasing.

Although detailed passenger ridership data for the station was not available for this analysis, it is a well-known fact that Gifu Station is a major terminal where the JR Tokaido Main Line and Takayama Main Line intersect, and it is adjacent to Meitetsu Gifu Station, which serves the Meitetsu Nagoya Main Line and Kakamigahara Line. This function as a transportation hub generates stable and robust demand for both rentals and home purchases.

From an investor's perspective, this means "low vacancy risk." With a diverse pool of potential tenants—including employees transferred to Nagoya, students of Gifu University and other nearby institutions, and those working for local companies—the area provides fertile ground for expecting stable income gains.

## 5. Gifu City's urban planning and future vision towards 2030

Discerning a city's long-term growth potential, beyond short-term market trends, is crucial for real estate investment. In this regard, Gifu City's urban plan offers investors a sense of security.

Gifu City has a clear vision for a "compact city centered on public transportation," aiming to concentrate urban functions in the city center and connect them to surrounding areas via public transit. This is a highly realistic and effective strategy for maintaining urban vitality in an era of population decline.

The area around Gifu Station, the focus of this report, is located right in that "central urban area." According to the data, this area is zoned as a "Commercial District," with a building coverage ratio of 80% and a floor area ratio of 400%, indicating high potential for land use. This suggests that the concentration of high-rise condominiums and office buildings may continue, raising expectations for a further increase in the area's value.

The sample data also includes various other zoning districts, such as "Category 1 Residential" and "Category 1 Low-Rise Exclusive Residential," showing that quiet residential neighborhoods spread out just a short distance from the city center. This balanced urban structure of "work, live, and shop" forms the foundation for Gifu City's sustainable development. Looking toward 2030, with the anticipated economic ripple effect that the opening of the Chuo Shinkansen will bring to Nagoya, Gifu City is poised to solidify its position as a key strategic base in the Tokai region.

## 6. Strengths and potential risks of real estate investment in the Gifu area

Based on the analysis so far, here is a summary of the strengths and risks of investing in real estate in the Gifu Station area.

[Strengths]
*   Excellent Access to Nagoya: A source of stable rental and housing demand.
*   Ongoing Redevelopment: Expectation of improved urban functions and rising asset values.
*   Relatively Affordable Property Prices: Potential for higher yields compared to Nagoya City.
*   Abundant Lifestyle Amenities: According to the data, there are at least 54 medical institutions, including "Watanabe Clinic" and "Yoshida Family Clinic," in the vicinity of the survey point, indicating a safe and convenient living environment.

[Potential Risks]
*   Hazard Risk: This is the most critical point to consider. The survey data shows a flood risk of "hasRisk: true," with a maximum expected inundation depth of "5 to 10 meters" (maxDepthRank: 4), which is a very high level. In areas close to the Nagara River and its tributaries, a proper assessment of flood risk is essential. When acquiring a property, it is imperative to check its precise location on a hazard map. Verifying the building's foundation height and waterproofing measures, and enrolling in a fire insurance policy that covers flood damage, should be considered absolute requirements. Fortunately, the risk of landslides is noted as "hasRisk: false," but preparations for flood risk cannot be neglected.
*   Areas with Insufficient Data: In this survey, information on elementary and junior high school districts (schools) could not be obtained. For investors targeting families, investigating the specific school district for each property is essential. It is important to recognize that public data is not always comprehensive, and on-the-ground research is ultimately crucial.
*   Tokai Earthquake Risk: Gifu Prefecture is a region that could be affected by the Nankai Trough megaquake. The seismic resistance of buildings, regardless of age, is one of the most important items to verify.

These risks can be managed with the right knowledge and countermeasures. The deciding factor for an investment will be whether the expected returns outweigh these understood risks.

## 7. Comparative analysis with competing areas (Ichinomiya, Ogaki, etc.)

From the perspective of a Nagoya commuter town, Ichinomiya City in Aichi Prefecture and Ogaki City in Gifu Prefecture are key competitors. What advantages does Gifu City hold over them?

*   Comparison with Ichinomiya: Ichinomiya Station also boasts excellent access to Nagoya Station, at about 10 minutes. However, being located within Aichi Prefecture, property prices tend to be higher than in Gifu City. For those prioritizing cost-performance, Gifu City will appear more attractive. Furthermore, as a prefectural capital, Gifu City has an advantage in terms of urban scale and the concentration of commercial facilities.

*   Comparison with Ogaki: Located west of Gifu City, Ogaki is about 30 minutes from Nagoya Station. Property prices are even more affordable than in Gifu, but the travel time to Nagoya is slightly longer. While Ogaki has its own economic zone with many corporate branches and factories, Gifu Station still leads in terms of overall urban strength and the scale of redevelopment.

In conclusion, Gifu City can be described as the area with the best balance of three key factors: "access to Nagoya," "property prices," and "concentration of urban functions." It is not as expensive as Ichinomiya, yet closer to Nagoya than Ogaki. This excellent positioning is the unique strength of the Gifu Station area.

This article has provided a multifaceted analysis of the real estate investment potential of the Gifu Station area, based on actual data as of July 2026.

In conclusion, the Gifu Station area is a highly promising investment target looking toward 2030, supported by improving urban functions from ongoing redevelopment and its unshakeable position as a key part of the Nagoya commuter belt. Whatever the strategy, calm, data-driven analysis and a cautious approach that directly confronts risk will be the keys to success.
