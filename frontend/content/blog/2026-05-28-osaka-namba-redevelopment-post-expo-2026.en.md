## 1. Introduction: Why is Osaka's Namba Back in the Spotlight?

In post-Expo Osaka, the central theme is how to sustain the city's appeal and connect it to further growth. Within this context, the Namba area is drawing renewed attention for reasons that go beyond its traditional role as a commercial and tourist hub, extending to the evolution of its complex urban functions.

First is the recovery of inbound tourism and the shift in its nature. In the wake of the pandemic and the Expo, tourist needs have clearly shifted from simply buying goods ("*mono-shohi*") to valuing unique local culture and experiences ("*koto-shohi*"). With the bustle of Dotonbori, the energy of Kuromon Market, and a deep-rooted culture of historic theaters, Namba possesses powerful content that can precisely meet this demand for experiences.

Second is the functional renewal of the area through large-scale redevelopment. The creation of Namba Parks South supplied high-quality office space to the Minami district, which had previously been considered lacking in business functions. This has attracted a new demographic of business workers, changing the weekday daytime population dynamics. This trend has the potential to create a virtuous cycle of "work, shop, and live," positively influencing surrounding commercial facilities and the residential market.

And third is the further strengthening of its transportation infrastructure. In addition to its strength as a direct access point to Kansai International Airport (KIX), the future opening of the Naniwasuji Line will solidify its position as a major transportation node connecting Osaka's north-south axis. These factors are intricately intertwined, acting as a driving force to boost the real estate value of the Namba area over the medium to long term.

## 2. Minami After the 2025 Osaka-Kansai Expo: A Strategy for Sustainable Growth

The 2025 Osaka-Kansai Expo was a golden opportunity to showcase the city of Osaka to the world once again. However, the true test begins now. The key question is how the Expo's legacy can be transformed into a sustainable engine for urban growth. Central to this is the further enhancement of Osaka's urban core, and the Minami-Namba area is poised to play a pivotal role.

In the greater metropolitan plan devised by Osaka Prefecture and City, Umekita is positioned as an international business and innovation hub, while Yumeshima is designated as an international tourism hub, including an integrated resort (IR). Namba is expected to connect these northern and western hubs, functioning as a massive gateway for visitors from across the region and as a center for "omotenashi" (hospitality), offering unique cultural and commercial experiences.

Capturing the increased demand for MICE (Meetings, Incentives, Conferences, and Exhibitions) and high-net-worth inbound tourists, spurred by the Expo, is a crucial post-Expo strategy. The presence of luxury hotels like the Centara Grand Hotel Osaka, adjacent to Namba Parks South, serves to accommodate this demand. Furthermore, as the number of free independent travelers (FITs) seeking deeper cultural experiences increases, Namba's diverse and eclectic charm will be a powerful tool for securing repeat visitors. Providing a high-quality environment for stays and consumption across the entire area, thereby increasing visitors' length of stay and spending per person, is essential for sustainable growth.

## 3. Progress of Major Redevelopment Projects: Namba Parks South and Surrounding Trends

The transformation of the Namba area is symbolized by Namba Parks South, which fully opened in July 2023. This project, led by Nankai Electric Railway, was not merely an expansion of a commercial facility but an initiative with enough impact to fundamentally change the area's value proposition.

Namba Parks South is composed of the state-of-the-art office building, Parks Tower, and the Thai luxury hotel brand, Centara Grand Hotel Osaka. This has supplied the Namba area with a large-scale, high-quality office stock that was previously lacking, attracting new businesses, particularly from the creative and IT industries. The influx of office workers has revitalized weekday lunch and after-work consumption, bringing new demand to surrounding restaurants and retail stores.

This development is not confined to the "point" of Namba Parks South. The momentum for new development is growing in the surrounding areas as well. For example, plans to renovate Nankai Namba Station and redevelop the public plaza in front of it are under consideration, promoting a shift towards a more pedestrian-friendly, comfortable, and attractive urban space. A short walk connects Namba to the Nipponbashi area, popular with tourists, and the Horie area, a hub for creators, with expectations for strengthened collaboration between these districts. As this "area-based" development centered on Namba progresses, it will enhance the walkability and appeal of the entire district, promising further appreciation in real estate values.

## 4. Enhanced Function as a Transportation Hub and its Impact on Inbound Tourism

The most fundamental and powerful factor supporting Namba's real estate value is its overwhelming transportation convenience. According to data from "Mekiki Research" dated today, May 28, 2026, the daily passenger count at the nearest station, Namba Station (Osaka Metro), reaches 331,190. When combined with the JR, Nankai, Kintetsu, and Hanshin lines, this number becomes even more immense, numerically confirming its status as one of the largest terminal stations in Western Japan.

In particular, Nankai Electric Railway's direct access to Kansai International Airport (KIX) has solidified Namba's position as the "Gateway to Osaka" for inbound tourists. It's approximately 40 minutes from the airport on the Limited Express Rapi:t. This accessibility is why many international visitors choose Namba as their initial base of stay.

Furthermore, its function as a transportation node is set to be strengthened even more in the future. The Naniwasuji Line, currently under construction and scheduled to open in 2031, will directly connect JR Namba Station and the provisional Nankai Shin-Namba Station with JR Osaka Station in the Umekita area. Once realized, this will link Osaka's long-separated Kita (north) and Minami (south) districts by a single rail line, dramatically improving mobility for both business and tourism. A new "golden route" will be born, leading from Shin-Osaka Station (where the Shinkansen stops) and the Umekita area to Namba, and onward to Kansai International Airport. This infrastructure development is an extremely reliable positive factor that will boost the real estate value of the Namba area over the medium to long term.

## 6. Office and Retail Rent Trends and Vacancy Rate Changes

The trends in the office and retail sectors are indispensable when discussing the real estate value of the Namba area. As work styles have diversified in the post-pandemic era, the office market has seen a clear polarization based on "location" and "quality." In this environment, the Namba area is leveraging its unique strengths.

State-of-the-art office buildings like Parks Tower in Namba Parks South are maintaining high occupancy rates, backed by their excellent transportation access and a vibrant surrounding environment that offers plenty of after-work entertainment. Companies are also increasingly choosing such attractive locations as part of their strategy to create "offices people want to come to" in order to attract top talent. As a result, the overall office rent market in the area has remained firm, with newly supplied properties, in particular, driving the market rates.

The retail market is also showing a powerful recovery, buoyed by the full return of inbound tourists. There is strong leasing demand for street-level stores on Namba's main thoroughfares and for spaces within major commercial facilities from drugstores, restaurants, and brand-name shops, keeping vacancy rates low and stable. In the future, demand is expected to grow not just for duty-free shops catering to tour groups, but also for tenants offering experiential content and high-value-added services. This has the potential to push rental levels even higher. In the retail market, where the vibrancy of an area directly translates into real estate value, Namba's potential remains exceptionally high.

## 7. Ripple Effects on the Residential Market: The Need for Proximity to Work and Potential for New Residential Areas

The concentration of office functions has a significant impact on the surrounding residential market. There is a growing movement among high-income business professionals working in the Namba area to seek housing "close to work." The desire to shorten commute times and enrich personal life is accelerating the trend of returning to the city center, which in turn supports demand for condominiums in the Namba area.

As the transaction data mentioned earlier shows, the high-rise condominium complex in the Minatomachi area is a symbol of this trend. In addition to great views and extensive shared facilities, its convenient location within walking distance of Namba Station is highly valued. In districts like Saiwaicho, which offer a slightly quieter living environment while still providing excellent access to the city center, a mix of new and old condominium types provides a range of options to suit diverse lifestyles.

However, there are points to be aware of when understanding the area's characteristics. The "Mekiki Research" survey for this report did not find data on public elementary or junior high schools in the immediate vicinity (`schools: null`). This suggests that the area has historically developed with a focus on commercial and business functions, and was not primarily envisioned as a residential area for families. Whether future urban development includes the supply of family-oriented housing and the establishment of educational and park facilities will be a key factor in determining the area's future demographic composition and sustainable growth.

On the other hand, the infrastructure supporting urban life is extremely well-developed. According to the data, there are 93 medical facilities in the vicinity of the survey point, offering easy access to a wide range of medical services, from large institutions like Tominaga Hospital to specialized clinics.

Finally, a factor that absolutely cannot be overlooked when considering investment or residency is disaster risk. According to hazard map data from the Geospatial Information Authority of Japan, this area is designated as a flood hazard zone (Rank 4) with an expected maximum inundation of 5 to 10 meters. This means there is a risk of serious flooding during typhoons or torrential rain. Fortunately, no landslide risk has been identified, but when acquiring property, it is essential to fully understand the risks by checking the building's own waterproofing and flood-prevention measures (e.g., placement of electrical equipment on higher floors), the local evacuation plan, and the detailed hazard map before making a decision.
