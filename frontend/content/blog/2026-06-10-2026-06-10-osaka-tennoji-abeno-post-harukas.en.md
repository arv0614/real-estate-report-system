It has been 12 years since the full opening of Abeno Harukas, a 300-meter-tall skyscraper complex, in March 2014. In that time, Osaka's Tennoji area has transformed from its former image as a simple terminal in the city's south into a vibrant hub for commerce, business, culture, and tourism, attracting people from both Japan and abroad. How has this dramatic change affected the area's real estate value?

This article provides a thorough analysis of the Tennoji real estate market based on the latest data as of June 2026. Using proprietary transaction data collected by "Mekiki Research," we will take a deep dive, from the perspective of a veteran real estate analyst, into land and property price trends, the potential of the rental market, and future prospects. Now in the wake of Expo 2025 Osaka, Kansai, we will assess the true value and future potential of this dynamic area.

## 1. Introduction: 12 Years After Abeno Harukas, A Look at the Tennoji & Abeno Area Today

The opening of Abeno Harukas was more than just the debut of a massive building. This landmark, which integrates diverse urban functions such as a department store, offices, a hotel, an art museum, and an observation deck, fundamentally changed the flow of people and the overall image of the area. Its achievement in establishing a new cultural and commercial zone in southern Osaka, distinct from the traditional business centers of Umeda (Kita) and Namba (Minami), cannot be overstated.

This transformation was accelerated by the success of "Tenshiba," the entrance area to Tennoji Park. Reopened in 2015, this spacious lawn plaza, complete with cafes, restaurants, and activity facilities, revamped the park's old image. It has become a true urban oasis, bustling with families and young people on weekends. This synergistic approach, combining both "hard" infrastructure (buildings) and "soft" utilization of space, has allowed the Tennoji and Abeno area to evolve from a "pass-through" town to a "destination" where people stay and enjoy themselves. In this report, we will unravel the impact of these changes on the real estate market with concrete data.

## 2. The Area's Transformation in Data: Transaction Trends Around Tennoji Station

Actual real estate transaction data is the most reliable indicator for objectively measuring an area's value. According to the latest data from Mekiki Research (as of June 10, 2026), the real estate market around Tennoji Station (Tennoji Ward, Osaka City) is showing extremely robust activity.

First, an impressive 2,613 transactions were recorded in the five-year period from 2021 to 2025. This substantial sample size speaks to the market's depth and high liquidity. Next, let's examine the key price indicators.

What's noteworthy is the significant discrepancy of about 16 million yen between the average transaction price (`avgTradePrice: 54408381`) and the median price (`medianTradePrice: 38000000`). This suggests that a few high-value properties for affluent buyers and large-scale transactions, with the highest recorded price reaching 3.2 billion yen, are pushing the average up. Therefore, for those looking for a typical pre-owned condominium or single-family home, the median price of 38 million yen serves as a more practical benchmark.

Looking at actual transaction examples reveals the area's diversity more clearly. For instance, in Ikutama-cho, a 30 m², 1LDK pre-owned condo built in 1979 was transacted for 8 million yen, while a property of the same size and layout in the same neighborhood, built in 2004, sold for 16 million yen, showing how prices fluctuate significantly with age. Furthermore, a 65 m² family-sized pre-owned condo built in 1985 in Ajihara-honmachi was sold for 25 million yen, and an 80 m² land and building package from 1991 in the Shitennoji area went for 40 million yen. This demonstrates that the market supplies properties that can accommodate a wide range of budgets and lifestyles, from single individuals to families. This breadth of pricing and property diversity is a major factor in Tennoji's appeal.

## 3. Rental Market Analysis: Rent Averages from Studio to Family-Sized Units

The vibrant sales market is directly supported by stable demand in the rental market. The outstanding transportation convenience of Tennoji Station, combined with the concentration of commercial facilities centered around Abeno Harukas, generates a wide range of rental needs from singles to families.

Mekiki Research's transaction data shows numerous examples of compact units like 1K (25 m²) and 1LDK (30 m²). This confirms the strong rental demand from working professionals and students, given the area's easy access to all parts of Osaka City via JR and subway lines. In particular, areas near the station are often zoned as "Commercial Districts," attracting tenants who prioritize convenience above all else.

On the other hand, shifting focus to areas slightly further from the station, like Shitennoji and Katsuyama, we can also find transactions for family-oriented properties, such as 65 m² "3LDK" units and 80 m² single-family homes. These areas are often designated as "Category 2 Residential Districts" and offer a calmer living environment, a step away from the bustle of the commercial zones. Tennoji Ward's long-standing reputation as an educational district also underpins rental demand from households with children.

From an investor's perspective, the Tennoji area is an attractive market that allows for diverse rental strategies. One could aim for stable income gain with a studio apartment near the station, or pursue long-term asset appreciation by renovating a family-sized property for rent. A median sales price of 38 million yen is a level that, while in a city center, still offers attractive investment opportunities.

## 4. The "Tenshiba" Success Story: Boosting Area Value and its Ripple Effects

The value of real estate is not determined solely by convenience or building specifications. Intangible assets like the "quality of the living environment" and the "city's brand image" significantly influence long-term property value. In this respect, the existence of "Tenshiba" is indispensable when discussing the enhancement of the Tennoji area's value.

The former Tennoji Park was, unfortunately, not a place citizens actively visited. However, through a redevelopment project that introduced private-sector vitality, "Tenshiba" was born—a fusion of an open lawn plaza and attractive commercial tenants. This dramatically improved the area's image and succeeded in attracting a new demographic. This success has had a positive impact on surrounding real estate values. Not only did the value of properties adjacent to the park increase, but being "close to Tenshiba" has become a powerful selling point for both rentals and sales.

This excellent living environment is also supported by data. The area around Tennoji Station is home to a total of 85 medical facilities (`medicalCount: 85`), from large institutions like the Osaka Metropolitan University Hospital to local clinics, providing a strong sense of security in case of emergencies.

Furthermore, the area's safety in terms of disaster prevention is a noteworthy feature. According to hazard map data from the Geospatial Information Authority of Japan referenced by Mekiki Research, the area around Tennoji Station currently has no identified risks of flooding (`flood.hasRisk: false`) or landslides (`landslide.hasRisk: false`). Its location on the solid ground of the Uemachi Plateau (Uemachi Daichi) contributes to its resilience against disasters. This sense of safety and security is a crucial factor when choosing a property and forms the foundation of the Tennoji area's solid asset value.

## 5. Strengths as a Transportation Hub and the Impact of Expo 2025

The most fundamental strength supporting the real estate value of the Tennoji area is its overwhelming transportation convenience. According to Mekiki Research data, the main Tennoji Station has 269,455 daily passengers on JR West lines alone (`dailyPassengers: 269455`), one of the highest figures in Osaka Prefecture.

*   JR West: Osaka Loop Line, Yamatoji Line (Kansai Main Line), Hanwa Line
*   Osaka Metro: Midosuji Line, Tanimachi Line
*   Kintetsu Railway: Minami Osaka Line (at Osaka-Abenobashi Station)
*   Hankai Tramway: Uemachi Line (at Tennoji-ekimae Station)

A terminal where this many lines converge is second only to Umeda and Namba in Osaka City. It offers direct access not only to the city center but also to Nara, Wakayama, and Kansai International Airport (KIX), giving it extremely high potential as a base for commuting, education, business, and tourism.

This transportation advantage saw its value further enhanced in the wake of Expo 2025 Osaka, Kansai. The Expo brought numerous visitors from Japan and abroad to Osaka, and Tennoji, with its excellent access from Kansai International Airport, functioned as the southern gateway to the city. This major event provided not only a short-term economic boost but also significantly raised the area's international profile, greatly contributing to attracting inbound tourists. The expected recovery and expansion of inbound tourism demand will be a key factor in pushing up the value of real estate, including hotels, commercial facilities, and short-term rental-eligible condos near the station, over the medium to long term.

## 6. Future Redevelopment Plans and Potential Risks (Aging Buildings, Competing Areas)

While the Tennoji area has undergone a major transformation with the success of Abeno Harukas and Tenshiba, its evolution is far from over. Future initiatives are expected to focus on sustainable city development, including further enhancements to Tennoji Park, strengthened collaboration with surrounding areas, and the renewal of aging infrastructure. These redevelopment plans hold the potential to elevate the area's value to the next level.

However, when considering an investment, it is crucial to also look at potential risks. The first is the presence of aging buildings scattered throughout the area. The transaction data sample for this report includes properties built in 1978 and 1985, from the era of old seismic resistance standards. These buildings may face challenges such as large-scale renovations or rebuilding in the future. When selecting a property, it is essential to meticulously check not only its age but also the status of the management association and its long-term repair fund plan.

The second risk is competition from other major areas within Osaka City. Large-scale urban development projects are underway across Osaka, including the Umeda area, which is evolving further with the opening of the Umekita 2nd Project, and the Bay Area, where an Integrated Resort (IR) project is planned. Amidst this competition, how the Tennoji area continues to refine and promote its unique charms will be key to determining its future asset value.

Additionally, specific public elementary and junior high school district information (`schools` was `null`) could not be obtained in this data survey. Tennoji Ward is known as one of Osaka's leading educational districts, and there is persistent real estate demand driven by specific school zones. When considering a family-oriented property, it is crucial to confirm the exact school district for the target property through the municipal website or a real estate agency.

## 7. An Investor's Perspective: Real Estate Investment Strategies and Properties to Watch in Tennoji & Abeno

Based on the analysis so far, here are three strategic approaches for real estate investors in the Tennoji area.

#### Strategy 1: Target Stable Demand with Compact Condos for Singles
Tennoji Station's outstanding transportation convenience creates stable rental demand from single professionals and students. Studio and 1LDK apartments within walking distance of the station have low vacancy risk and can be expected to generate stable income gain over the long term. The data shows instances of 30 m² 1LDK units transacting in the 8-million-yen range, suggesting it's possible to start investing with a relatively small amount of capital.

#### Strategy 2: Create Added Value by Renovating Family-Sized Properties
In areas with a rich history and calm residential atmosphere, such as around Shitennoji, there is strong demand from families. By acquiring an older single-family home or condominium and renovating it with modern layouts and amenities, it's possible to create significant added value. This strategy can be effective for aiming for capital gains through resale or for renting out at a higher-than-market rate.

#### Strategy 3: Focus on Future Potential with Development/Redevelopment in High Floor-Area Ratio Zones
The "Commercial Districts" around Tennoji Station often have high floor-area ratios (FAR), allowing for maximum utilization of land potential. According to Mekiki Research's location data, high building efficiencies such as a building-coverage ratio of 80% and a floor-area ratio of 400% (`environment.zoning`) are permitted around the station. Some transaction examples are found in areas with FARs reaching 600% or 800%. Such land is highly attractive as development sites for small-scale hotels anticipating future inbound demand or for highly profitable rental apartment buildings. However, this approach requires professional judgment based on a detailed investigation of legal regulations and the surrounding environment.

Regardless of the strategy, the key to success lies in carefully examining the individual factors of a property (location, age, management condition) and comparing it with surrounding transaction examples.

## 8. Conclusion: A Key Hub in Southern Osaka with Continued Growth Expected Beyond 2026

In the 12 years since the opening of Abeno Harukas, the Tennoji and Abeno area has undergone a dramatic evolution in both its "hard" infrastructure and "soft" amenities, growing into one of Osaka's most attractive districts. Its value is clearly reflected in the market data from Mekiki Research, which shows a vibrant and deep market with 2,613 transactions and a median price of 38 million yen.

The combination of outstanding transportation convenience, a concentration of commercial and cultural functions, a high-quality living environment symbolized by "Tenshiba," and resilience to disasters creates a solid foundation for the area's real estate value. Its international profile was boosted by Expo 2025 Osaka, Kansai, and the full-scale recovery of inbound tourism demand will serve as a significant tailwind, pushing the area's value even higher.

Of course, challenges such as the renewal of aging buildings and competition with other areas exist. However, this district has more than enough potential and future promise to overcome them. From the perspectives of commerce, residence, and investment, the Tennoji and Abeno area will undoubtedly remain a key hub driving Osaka's real estate market in 2026 and beyond.
