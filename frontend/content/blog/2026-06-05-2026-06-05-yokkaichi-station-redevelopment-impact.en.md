## 1. Introduction: The Industrial City of Yokkaichi Faces a Period of Transformation

Yokkaichi City, the most populous city in Mie Prefecture, developed into one of Japan's leading industrial zones after World War II, centered around its petrochemical complexes. While this industrial base remains the backbone of the city's economy, Yokkaichi has faced challenges such as a decline in the vitality of its city center and a shrinking population, amidst national trends of shifting industrial structures and a return to urban centers.

The area around Kintetsu-Yokkaichi Station, the city's core, has a concentration of commercial and public facilities but has been plagued by issues like aging buildings, confusing pedestrian routes, and scattered bus stops. The Kintetsu-Yokkaichi Station Area Redevelopment Project was conceived to solve these problems and transform the area into a "city of choice"—a place where people can gather, interact, and live comfortably.

The project is built on three pillars: strengthening its function as a transportation hub, introducing new commercial and business functions, and creating an attractive urban space. As of 2026, as the project's form gradually takes shape, signs of change are beginning to appear in the real estate market. In this report, we will conduct a multifaceted analysis of the value created by this historic redevelopment and provide guidance for future investment strategies.

## 2. Overview of the Kintetsu-Yokkaichi Station Area Redevelopment Project and Progress as of 2026

The Kintetsu-Yokkaichi Station Area Redevelopment Project consists of two main phases: "Development of a Transportation Hub" and "Creation of a New Hub of Activity." As of June 2026, construction of the project's core element, the "Busta Yokkaichi" (provisional name) transportation terminal, is in full swing, and plans for an adjacent new mixed-use building are also taking concrete form. The city's very framework is in the process of a major overhaul.

The project's overall vision is as follows:

*   Strengthening the Transportation Hub Function:
    *   Development of "Busta Yokkaichi" (provisional name): Consolidating local bus, highway bus, and taxi stands scattered around the station to dramatically improve transfer convenience with rail services.
    *   Redevelopment and Extension of Pedestrian Decks: Connecting the station with surrounding facilities at the second-floor level to create a safe and comfortable pedestrian network.
*   Creation of a Hub of Activity:
    *   Construction of a New Mixed-Use Building: A landmark building featuring commercial facilities, offices, a hotel, and urban-style residences.
    *   Redevelopment of the Station Plaza: Creating an open and inviting plaza space for events and public relaxation.

These plans aim to do more than simply update infrastructure. The goal is to connect the previously isolated station, commercial facilities, and public buildings, revitalizing the entire city center. As of 2026, land acquisition and demolition of existing buildings are underway in some areas, raising public expectations. This momentum is already beginning to influence land and transaction prices, reflecting anticipation of "future value" in the real estate market.

## 3. Key Project Breakdown: The Roles of "Busta Yokkaichi" and the New Mixed-Use Building

The success of the redevelopment hinges on two key components: "Busta Yokkaichi" and the "New Mixed-Use Building."

"Busta Yokkaichi" will serve as the city's transportation nexus. According to data from Mekiki Research, Kintetsu-Yokkaichi Station is one of the prefecture's major terminals, with 43,417 passengers entering and exiting daily. However, its bus stops have historically been dispersed, making transfers inconvenient. The development of Busta will streamline connections between trains and buses, improving not only intra-city travel but also enhancing the station's status as a gateway to Nagoya and the Kansai region. This is expected to increase the gravitational pull of the station area, stimulating demand for commercial facilities and offices. Improved transportation convenience is one of the most direct factors driving up real estate values.

Meanwhile, the new mixed-use building will add new functions and appeal to the city. Plans call for trendy retail tenants on the lower floors, offices that will generate new employment on the middle floors, and a hotel and residences on the upper floors. In particular, the supply of urban-style residences (apartments) that promote a "live-work-play" lifestyle is expected to draw back residents who previously preferred suburban single-family homes or commuted from the Nagoya area. This will increase the nighttime population and contribute to a more 24/7 city center.

Through the synergy of these two projects, the Kintetsu-Yokkaichi Station area will transform its character from a mere transit point into a destination in itself.

Focusing on the Kintetsu-Yokkaichi Station area, the epicenter of the redevelopment, its high potential is evident in the data. The area is zoned as a "Neighborhood Commercial Zone," which allows for a high land-use efficiency with an 80% building coverage ratio (kenpeiritsu) and a 300% floor area ratio (yosekiritsu). This makes it easier to construct high-rise buildings and apartments, a crucial factor supporting the financial viability of the redevelopment project. This high potential is precisely what creates fertile ground for attracting large-scale private investment.

## 5. Predicting Demographic Shifts: Can Redevelopment Make Yokkaichi a "City of Choice"?

Ultimately, it is "population" that sustains long-term real estate value. Can the redevelopment project have a positive impact on Yokkaichi's demographics?

In short, the potential is certainly there. If the redevelopment improves convenience and residential comfort around the station, it is expected to retain younger and child-rearing generations who were previously moving out of the city, and even attract new residents from outside.

A key reason for this is the well-developed surrounding environment. According to Mekiki Research data, there are 29 medical institutions concentrated around Kintetsu-Yokkaichi Station, providing a secure living environment. The area is also zoned for Hamada Elementary School and Chubu Junior High School, indicating a stable educational environment. This robust living infrastructure is a major draw, especially for families.

Furthermore, as mentioned earlier, the convenience of Kintetsu-Yokkaichi Station, used by over 40,000 people daily, is also attractive to those who commute to the Nagoya area for work or school. If high-quality urban residences are supplied in front of the station, it will allow residents to enjoy the convenience of a "door-to-door commute to Nagoya in under an hour" while securing a comfortable living environment at a more affordable price than in Nagoya city itself. This could be a powerful hook to attract new residents from a wider region.

The redevelopment is not just about giving the city a facelift; it presents new lifestyle choices and could be a pivotal moment in transforming Yokkaichi into a "desirable city" or a "city of choice."

## 6. Shifting Industrial Structure and Real Estate Needs: Potential to Move Beyond Dependence on Industrial Complexes

Yokkaichi's economy has long been supported by the manufacturing industry, symbolized by its industrial complexes. However, for sustainable urban management, diversification of the industrial structure is essential. The redevelopment holds the potential to play a part in this shift.

The inclusion of modern office floors in the new mixed-use building is critically important. This will create an environment to attract IT companies, service industries, and corporate satellite offices, for which there was previously no suitable space in the city. By leveraging its prime location with good access from Nagoya, attracting new businesses is a very real possibility.

The arrival of new companies creates new jobs and draws high-quality talent to the city. This, in turn, will generate new demand in the real estate market. For example, it will spur diverse housing needs, including rental apartments for employees on solo assignment and young professionals, luxury rentals for executives, and condominiums for employees to purchase.

Moreover, an increase in office workers will vitalize weekday lunch and after-work consumption, boosting sales at nearby restaurants and retail stores. This will enhance the value of commercial real estate in the form of higher rents for retail properties and lower vacancy rates.

In this way, the redevelopment has the potential to foster a virtuous cycle where a shift in industrial structure stimulates new real estate demand.

## 7. Areas and Property Types for Investors to Watch (Residential, Commercial, Office)

Given these changes, where and what types of properties should real estate investors focus on in Yokkaichi City?

1. Residential Properties
The most direct beneficiaries of the redevelopment will be apartments within walking distance of Kintetsu-Yokkaichi Station. Demand is expected to be high for 1LDK to 2LDK units catering to singles and DINKs (Dual Income, No Kids), as well as 3LDK+ units for families, serving the new resident demographic. In addition to new constructions, existing apartments with renovation potential are also attractive investment targets.

2. Commercial (Retail) Properties
Street-level shops facing the new station plaza and pedestrian decks, where new streams of people will flow, are expected to see their value as prime commercial real estate increase. Demand for lifestyle-oriented tenants such as cafes, restaurants, and clinics will likely remain solid. The zoning as a "Neighborhood Commercial Zone" is another strength, as it permits a wide variety of business types.

3. Office Properties
The office floors in the new mixed-use building are expected to sit at the top of the city's office building hierarchy. While direct investment may be difficult for individual investors, the emergence of such Class A office space is likely to have a ripple effect, boosting the value and rent levels of surrounding small and mid-sized office buildings.

Investment Caveat: Checking Hazard Risks
On the other hand, understanding risks is essential for making investment decisions. According to hazard data from Mekiki Research, the area around Kintetsu-Yokkaichi Station has a flood risk with projected inundation depths of up to 3-5 meters. Fortunately, no landslide risks have been identified, but flood mitigation is a must. It is crucial to consider concrete risk-hedging measures, such as verifying that a target property has implemented appropriate flood countermeasures (e.g., elevating the building or installing flood-proofing equipment) or choosing upper floors in the case of an apartment building.

In 2026, the Kintetsu-Yokkaichi Station Area Redevelopment is steadily progressing as a major project that will significantly shape the future of the industrial city of Yokkaichi. The enhancement of its transportation hub function and the introduction of new urban features will change the flow of people, create new demand, and bring about a structural shift in the real estate market.

Transaction data from 2021 to 2025 shows a diverse range of property prices across the city, but as the redevelopment progresses, it is predicted that the value of the area around the station will rise selectively. The potential of a station used by over 40,000 people daily, the land-use freedom of an 80% building coverage ratio and 300% floor area ratio, and the well-developed living infrastructure all support this promising future.

For investors, an excellent opportunity to benefit from the value enhancement driven by redevelopment is emerging. However, risks such as the flood hazard also exist. The key is to combine macro-level development plans with micro-level property data to calmly assess the balance of risk and return.

Yokkaichi City is in the midst of a transformation. At this historic turning point, deep insights based on accurate data will be the key to successful real estate investment.
